The Evolution of the Bicycle

In 1817 Baron von Drais invented a walking machine that would help him get around the royal gardens faster the bicycle covered 13 kilometres in less than an hour.

Constructed almost entirely of wood, the draisine weighed 22 kg and had no pedals but what it lacked in practicality, it made up for with speed.

Denis Johnson made refinements to this model and during the summer of 1819 the “hobby-horse” became the craze and fashion in London society.
